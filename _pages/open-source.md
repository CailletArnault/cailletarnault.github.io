# Open Source

I am an advocate for **open science**. During my academic journey, I **contributed datasets and code** around motoneuron electrophysiology, cross-session/task high-density surface EMG, motor unit physiology and activity, and neuromuscular modelling. I now **push open-science initiatives** through my dual role as CSO of Yneuro and Honorary Research Officer at Imperial College London: developing and maintaining code libraries, organizing open competitions, and building bridges between academia and industry. I strongly believe that science progresses when the ecosystem can work hand-in-hand around shared tools, open benchmarks, and reusable resources on a large scale, industry and academia alike.

## Organizing Open Challenges

I co-lead with [Bruno Aristimunha](https://bruaristimunha.github.io/) the organization of the [Neural Interface 2026 EEG-EMG Competition](https://neuralinterface26.github.io/index.html). **Yneuro** is the lead organizer in collaboration with **Inria**, **UCSD**, and **Meta**.

The competition, centered around the topic of EEG Foundational Models for addressing domain shifts (subject, session, emotions, context, hardware) connects academic labs, open-source infrastructure, and industry research teams across four tracks:

- **EEG-to-Image** with the Meta FAIR Brain & AI team and Alljoined
- **BCI Decoding** with Inria LaBRI
- **EEG to Sleep Onset** with InteraXon Inc.
- **EMG-to-Text** with Meta Reality Labs and Imperial College London

## Supporting Open-Source Infrastructure

- **[Braindecode](https://braindecode.org/stable/index.html)** — Building a Yneuro x Inria Paris-Saclay x LISN collaboration to contribute to and sponsor development and maintenance of Braindecode.
- **[Codabench](https://www.codabench.org/)** — Building the synergy between the Codabench team at Paris-Saclay and AWS/Deloitte support to scale the competition platform.
- **MNE-EMG** — Initiating, with [Pranav Mamidanna](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=5x1hXY8AAAAJ&hl=en), a central Python package for EMG signal processing and analysis within the [MNE-Python](https://mne.tools/stable/index.html) ecosystem.

## Developing Python Packages

- **MUniverse** — Simulation and benchmarking suite for motor unit decomposition, spanning synthetic, hybrid, and experimental EMG with ground-truth motor unit spikes.  
  [datasetcatalog.nlm.nih.gov/dataset?q=0002130537](https://github.com/dfarinagroup/muniverse)
- **Motoneuron-Driven Neuromuscular Model** — Subject-specific neuromuscular modelling with motor unit resolution.  
  [github.com/ArnaultCAILLET/MN-driven-Neuromuscular-Model-with-motor-unit-resolution](https://github.com/ArnaultCAILLET/MN-driven-Neuromuscular-Model-with-motor-unit-resolution)
- **NeuroMotion Simulator** — Dynamic, motion-driven EMG signal generation with neuromechanical and deep learning models.  
  [github.com/shihan-ma/NeuroMotion](https://github.com/shihan-ma/NeuroMotion)
- **Data Augmentation for Motoneuron Pool Reconstruction** — EMG-based motoneuron pool reconstruction with data augmentation.  
  [github.com/ArnaultCAILLET/Caillet-et-al-2022-PLOS_Comput_Biol](https://github.com/ArnaultCAILLET/Caillet-et-al-2022-PLOS_Comput_Biol)

## Releasing Data

- **High-Density EMG Dataset, 16 participants** — Signals and manually edited spike trains from two muscles, with 2000+ identified motor units.  
  [figshare.com/articles/dataset/24640944/1](https://figshare.com/articles/dataset/24640944/1)
- **High-Density EMG Dataset, 6 participants** — Signals and manually edited spike trains from one muscle, with 500+ identified motor units.  
  [doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.22149287](https://doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.22149287)
- **Medical Imaging & Musculoskeletal Model Dataset** — Segmented anatomical volumes and subject-specific musculoskeletal models for neuromuscular simulations.  
  [zenodo.org/records/10069266](https://zenodo.org/records/10069266)
- **Cat & Rat Motoneuron Electrophysiology Dataset** — Experimental motoneuron electrophysiology data for cross-species modelling.  
  [elifesciences.org/articles/76489/figures](https://elifesciences.org/articles/76489/figures#content)
